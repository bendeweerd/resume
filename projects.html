Calvin University Robotic Football Team

Calvin University Mars Rover Design Team

I was an active member of Calvin's Mars Rover Team. I designed, modeled, and 3D printed the "wrist" of the rover arm, achieving the 4th and 5th degrees of freedom. I was also responsible for designing and 3D printing parts for other parts of the rover. The Rover team was restructured and took on a new challenge as the Robotic Football Team.

Robotic Arm

I am currently designing and building my own robotic arm. While it is a work in progress, I have designed and constructed custom NEMA 17 gearboxes and a slew bearing for the arm's base. I plan to control the arm with a raspberry pi and Arduino Due, which has a higher clock speed that will allow me to smoothly and simultaneously control the five stepper motors.

Luminous Display Pedestal

This was a hybrid woodshop and Arduino/programming project. I constructed the table in the FHS woodshop, then added the LED strips and controller that I programmed at home. The Arduino Sketch for the project showcases some of my earliest programming experience (or lack thereof).

Dorm Room RGB Lights

I programmed these lights for my dorm room. They display 8 different effects and are driven by an Arduino. Much of the code was taken from my Luminous Display Pedestal project, with modifications for more effects and modified controls.

Falcon Heavy Model
